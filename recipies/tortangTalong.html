Tortang Talong

Tortang talong, also known as eggplant omelette, is an omelette or fritter from Filipino cuisine made by pan-frying grilled whole eggplants dipped in an egg mixture. It is a popular breakfast and lunch meal in the Philippines.

Ingredients:
  • 1 Eggplant or more; depending on how many you want to make.
  • 1 Egg or more
  • Chopped Onion
  • Flour or Cornstarch
  • salt and/or MSG for taste
  • 25ml of cooking oil
Steps:
  1. Boil the eggplant/s until soft, use a fork and stab through the eggplant to test.
  2. Get the eggplant out of the boiling water, and prepare the other ingredients.
  3. Depeding on your preference, peel off the skin of the eggplant, brush it with the fork, and watch out for worms. Remove the stem in your preference.
  4. Mix the eggplant in a bowl, together with an egg, chopped onions, a teaspoon of cornstarch/flour, and some seasoning/salt for taste.
  5. Heat a pan for 1200Â°F, or medium heat. Add 25ml of cooking oil
  6. Cook the mixture into the pan, wait until the sides are brown, and carefully flip the eggplant.
  7. Wait for it until the eggplant turns golden brown.
  8. Take off the eggplant in the pan, and serve while hot.
